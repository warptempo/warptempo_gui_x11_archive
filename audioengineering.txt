In fact, I didn't know about Hummel until after I completed a first draft of the 40th, of which the 1st movement has mostly remained unchanged, and the 4th was updated in the quiet parts but not much in the louder parts. The 2nd mvt was significantly sped up, however, as was the menuetto in the 3rd mvt. 
https://digibib.mozarteum.at/ismretroverbund/search?operation=searchRetrieve&query=bib.personalName%3D%22Hummel%2C%20Johann%20Nepomuk%22

Point being, there seems to be a "right" way to play this music, encoded into the music itself - if measure A is played at X speed / volume / etc, then measure B can only be correctly played in approx. Y speed / volume / etc. Of course, the mastering part is purely my creative input, to push the boundary of how the sound can be updated for modern and future technologies.
